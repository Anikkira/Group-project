Limitation

Limitation

Limitation 1.
Lack of practical AI

AI chatbots lack the ability to understand complex human emotions. They can only provide answers based on the data they have been trained on and cannot learn new information on their own. They may struggle with more complex issues that require human intervention. AI chatbots are built to mimic human interactions, but they lack the practical intelligence to understand complex situations or provide personalized attention. They can handle simple and repetitive tasks, but when it comes to addressing complex issues, chatbots often fall short.


Limitation 2.
Unawareness of Context

The chatbot models don't have human-level understanding. Although chatbot models have made significant strides in language processing and generation, they are still limited by their lack of true comprehension and contextual understanding.


Limitation 3.
Platform-Dependent Operation

Chatbots are often designed to work on specific platforms, such as Facebook Messenger or Slack, which can limit their reach and effectiveness. The platform-dependent operation can limit the chatbots' ability to reach a wider audience and provide support to all users.
